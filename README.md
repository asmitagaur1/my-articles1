<p>Entrance Exams in Online Degree Courses</p>
<p>Are Entrance Exams Mandatory?</p>
<p>Unlike many traditional campus-based programs, entrance exams are not always mandatory for online degree courses. Institutions may adopt different admission models based on academic goals and learner demographics.</p>
<p>Common admission approaches include:</p>
<ul>

<li>Profile-based evaluation for working professionals<br /> <br /> </li>
</ul>
<p>For online MBA programs, some universities conduct internal assessments to evaluate analytical, communication, and quantitative skills.</p>
<p>Types of Assessments Used for Admission</p>

<p>Evaluation and Assessment Methods in Online Degree Courses</p>
<p>Continuous Evaluation Model</p>
<p>One of the defining features of online education is the emphasis on continuous assessment rather than single end-term examinations. Evaluation frameworks are designed to measure learning outcomes progressively.</p>
<p>Assessment components commonly include:</p>

<li>Assignments and written submissions</li>
<li>Case study analysis</li>
<li>Group projects and presentations</li>
</ul>
<p>This model supports consistent learning and reduces performance pressure.</p>
<p>Proctored Online Examinations</p>

</ul>
